The leading crypto trends 2023: 4 trends to keep an eye

Government adoption of crypto

Crypto regulations already became stricter in 2022. The collapse of FTX focused public attention on the need for greater control of crypto, so government intervention in cryptocurrencies is expected to be even more visible in the future. Meanwhile, in 2023, more central bank cryptocurrencies may be launched, becoming one of the cryptocurrency trends. The introduction of CBDCs in 2023 for GBP, USD, and EUR is highly anticipated, and the European government is serious about new assets. At the High Level Conference on the Digital Euro, ECB President Christine Lagarde declared: "The digital euro is more than just a Eurosystem initiative, it is a common European project".

AI will also affect cryptocurrencies

It is safe to say that artificial intelligence will be the central theme of 2023 in the IT and cryptocurrency niches. Several crypto tokens linked to this area have grown by tens of percent since the beginning of the year due to the rapid advancement of neural networks that process data (text, audio, and video). Examples of such projects are Fetch.AI and SingularityNET, blockchain-based infrastructure for Web2 and Web3 AI applications. By using an open-source, decentralized machine learning platform, users can access Fetch.AI's secure datasets. At the same time, SingularityNET aims to provide an AI marketplace where participants can create, share, and monetize AI services at scale.

One more Ethereum upgrade

The Merge already made some noise last year. Another update awaits this year - Shanghai. It is expected to launch in March. The main feature of Shanghai will be the ability to withdraw staked ETH. Since the September Merge went live, users can deposit existing ETH with the Ethereum network to verify Ethereum transactions and generate new ETH.

However, it still hasn't been possible to withdraw any staked or newly generated ETH.

In March, the network's Shanghai upgrade will make $20 billion worth of staked ETH available to users.

The Ethereum developers also discussed a second hard fork planned after Shanghai sometime in the third quarter of 2023, which will introduce proto-danksharding or EIP 4844. This update aims to improve the network's ability to process more transactions.

EIP 4844 will introduce a new type of transaction format to Ethereum called "shard blob transactions", which will allow Ethereum nodes to temporarily store and access data off-chain to scale the blockchain applications.

Putting NFTs to work in the real world

Since the adoption of non-fungible tokens (NFTs), there has been a surge in interest in crypto games and collectibles. These new assets have changed the way digital assets can be used.
As a result, NFTs are now focused on real-world utility, offering a more sustainable way to develop.

Large organizations provide their clients with NFT certificates instead of the usual contracts. NFT projects like BAGC focus on physical things like clothes, souvenirs, and offline communities.

Despite the rough year crypto had in 2022, the digital currency industry is continually growing and evolving, and the upcoming year will provide experienced investors and newcomers with innovative and lucrative opportunities.
